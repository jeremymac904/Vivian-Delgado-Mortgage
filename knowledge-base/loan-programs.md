# Loan Program Summaries

Conventional loans fit buyers with strong credit and stable income. They offer flexibility and long term cost control.

FHA loans help buyers with low down payment needs. They include mortgage insurance and require a clear long term plan.

VA loans serve eligible veterans with zero down and no monthly mortgage insurance. Appraisal standards apply.

USDA loans support eligible areas with zero down. Income and location limits apply.

Jumbo loans serve higher loan amounts with lender specific pricing. Assets and reserves matter.

Refinance should focus on net benefit, break even math, and long term savings.

DSCR loans qualify on property cash flow, not personal income. Rents, reserves, and leverage drive pricing.

Fix and flip loans fund purchase and rehab with staged draws. Clear scope and exit plans matter.

Ground up construction loans fund new builds with draw schedules and strict documentation.

FHA 203k loans combine purchase and rehab into one mortgage. Contractor and timeline rules apply.

HomeReady and Home Possible are conventional options with income limits and lower down payments.

ITIN loans use alternative documentation for ITIN borrowers. Down payments and reserves are higher.

Bank statement loans qualify income using deposits. Clean statements and steady cash flow are key.

HELOCs provide flexible equity access. Terms and rates differ for primary and investment properties.
